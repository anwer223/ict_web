03
Team Meeting

Identifying Problems and Solutions

February 22, 2026

Problem 1: Limited access to existing equipment.

Solution 1: Develop a remote access system to allow multiple students to control experiments online.

Problem 2: Complete absence of physical equipment.

Solution 2: Create a fully virtual simulation with 3D visualization and real-time data graphing.

Both solutions will be integrated into a flexible platform that adapts to the resources of each institution.

04
Supervisor Meeting

Conclusion of Article Reading Phase

March 28, 2026 · With Mr. Kerrouchi & Mr. Tarek

This meeting marked the conclusion of the article reading phase. We presented our summaries and discussed how each article contributed to our understanding of the problem and potential solutions.

Key takeaways from the literature:

  • Low-cost ESP32-based platforms are feasible for physics education.
  • Remote and virtual labs can increase accessibility and reduce costs.
  • Open-source sensor systems provide a solid foundation for building our own solution.
05
Literature Review

Scientific Foundations – Three Key Articles

March 2026

01. Development of an Educational Low‑Cost and ESP32‑Based Platform for Fundamental Physics
ENSTA, Algeria – Implementation of a low‑cost modular and open‑source didactic platform using an ESP32 board, designed for physics experiments in engineering training.

02. Low‑Cost Virtual Physics Lab for Moroccan Universities
EXPRES Project (2016–2018), Morocco – A low‑cost physics experiment simulation platform based on JavaScript, integrated into an interactive learning management system.

03. Open‑source Sensors System for Simple Physics Experiments
Universidad de Valladolid, Spain – Design and construction of a low‑cost platform combining different sensors for simple physics experiments. This article proposes a solution that is closest to ours.

These articles formed the foundation of our project and guided our choice of technology and approach.

06
Team Meeting

Choosing the First Experiment – Rheographic Tank

avril 25, 2026

The team evaluated several potential physics experiments for the prototype. After discussion, we selected the Rheographic Tank experiment.

This experiment involves mapping electric fields between charged electrodes in a conductive medium. We chose it because it allows students to visualize abstract concepts like equipotential lines and electric field vectors, directly addressing the "Difficulty Understanding" problem identified earlier. This choice also leverages the team's skills in 3D simulation and sensor data acquisition.

07
Implementation Planning

Planning the Rheographic Experiment Implementation

May 5, 2026

Following the selection of the rheographic tank, we held a dedicated meeting to define how we will build and automate this experiment. The team outlined the following technical approach:

  • Hardware Design: Build a small acrylic tank with two parallel metal plates serving as electrodes.
  • Measurement System: Use a movable probe connected to an ADS1115 ADC to measure voltage at different points in the water.
  • Actuation: Implement a stepper motor system to precisely position the probe across the tank.
  • Control & Acquisition: Use an ESP32 to control the stepper motors, read the ADC, and manage the data flow.
  • Visualization: Develop a 3D simulation using Three.js to display equipotential lines and the electric field in real-time.
  • Data Pipeline: Transmit measurements via MQTT to ThingsBoard for storage, visualization, and further analysis.

This plan provides a clear roadmap for moving from the design phase to the implementation phase, combining hardware, firmware, and software development.
